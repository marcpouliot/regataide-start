User Guide — Regataide Start

V1 using phone GPS

Back to app Français

Purpose

Regataide Start helps a sailor manage the approach to the starting line using the phone GPS. It is not an official judging tool.

The app calculates perpendicular distance to line A-B, line side, time remaining, and ideal speed to reach the line at the next full minute.

Line convention

  • A = committee boat.
  • B = starting buoy.
  • Buoy to port = when crossing, the boat leaves the buoy on port.
  • Buoy to starboard = when crossing, the boat leaves the buoy on starboard.

The buffer avoids treating a few meters of GPS error as a definite crossing.

Quick start

  1. Open the app over HTTPS on the phone.
  2. Tap Enable GPS and accept permission.
  3. Enter the coordinates for committee boat A and buoy B.
  4. Choose Buoy to port or Buoy to starboard.
  5. Choose the speed unit: knots or miles/hour.
  6. Tap SYNC 5:00 at the five-minute signal, or enter the official start time and tap Arm this time.

Example 1 — Sync at the five-minute signal

At the official 5:00 signal, tap SYNC 5:00.

Time remaining: 4:18
Target: 4:00
Available time: 18 s
Line distance: 42 m
Ideal speed: 4.5 kt

Meaning: to cross the line at 4:00, aim for about 4.5 knots toward the line.

Example 2 — Fixed official start time

The race starts at 13:35:00. Enter 13:35:00 under “Official start today”, then tap Arm this time.

The app uses the phone clock. Make sure the phone time is correct.

Example 3 — Wrong side of the line

If the state shows Course side / line crossed or RETURN, the app believes the boat is on the wrong side of the starting line. Return to the pre-start side before using the calculation again.

Testing tips

  • First test walking or driving slowly in an open area.
  • Use a 10 to 15 m buffer with phone GPS.
  • Watch the displayed GPS accuracy. If it says ±20 m, results will be approximate.
  • Use miles/hour for easier land testing. Use knots for sailing.

Distance mode

Perpendicular: measures the gap to line A-B. Recommended for starting.

Center: measures distance to the middle of the line. Useful for comparison and debugging.
